Every channel you're on charges you before a sale happens

If you're running a Shopify store or selling on Amazon, you already know what the past few years have felt like. The same budget buys less reach. The same ad spend acquires fewer customers. You're not doing anything wrong. The channels themselves are getting more competitive and more expensive, and there is no ceiling in sight.

Meta wants more money per impression. Amazon wants more per click. Google wants more per visit. Every channel you rely on has the same model: you pay upfront, they keep the traffic, and you hope the math works out on the other side.

TikTok Shop works differently. You pay after the sale happens, not before. Creators post videos featuring your product, those videos drive purchases, and you pay a commission on what actually converts. No upfront spend. No cost-per-click. No impression fees.

"The question is not whether TikTok Shop fits your brand. The question is whether you can afford to let your competitors have it to themselves."
