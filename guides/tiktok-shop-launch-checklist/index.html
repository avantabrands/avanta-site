TikTok Shop launch checklist: every step before, during, and after go-live.

Most TikTok Shop launches underperform because critical work gets skipped in the rush to go live. This checklist covers every phase — seller center setup through your first 90-day review — so nothing gets missed and your first creator signals land on a properly prepared account.

  • Pre-launch: seller center and product setup (30+ items)
  • Creator seeding checklist before go-live
  • Go-live day actions and what to hold off on
  • Week 1–2 optimization actions
  • Week 3–4: GMV Max activation criteria
  • First 90-day monthly review framework

Why sequence matters more than speed

A strong TikTok Shop launch is not about being first to publish. It is about making sure the listing, the logistics, and the creator ecosystem are all prepared to convert when traffic arrives. We have seen brands drive thousands of views in the first week with nothing to show for it in sales because the listing was weak, inventory was thin, or the affiliate program was not properly configured.

The beauty brand we scaled to $672K GMV, 41,330 orders, and a 7.49 ROAS in one year did not succeed because the launch was fast. It succeeded because the foundation was correct before the first creator video posted. That foundation is what this checklist is designed to build.

Work through this in order. Each phase depends on the one before it.

Phase 1: Pre-launch setup checklist (30+ items)

This phase covers everything from seller center account creation through listing readiness. Complete all of these before reaching out to a single creator.

Seller center and account verification

  • Register at seller-us.tiktok.com using a verified corporate email and phone number
  • Submit business registration documents (EIN or state registration, LLC or corp)
  • Upload government-issued ID for the account owner — name must match business docs exactly
  • Provide business bank account details for payout setup — bank name must match legal business name
  • Verify that business address matches across all documents (mismatches are the top rejection reason)
  • Allow 1–5 business days for approval; do not begin product setup until the account is confirmed
  • Complete the TikTok Shop seller agreement and review payout schedule and fee structure
  • Link your brand's TikTok business profile to the seller account under Linked Accounts

Product category approval and compliance

  • Identify which product categories require additional approval (beauty, supplements, electronics, food)
  • Submit category approval requests with required certifications (FDA, lab reports, safety data sheets)
  • Confirm product compliance with TikTok's prohibited and restricted products policy
  • For supplements: ensure label claims are compliant and have documentation ready for review
  • Confirm your hero SKU is eligible for all planned shipping methods before listing it

Inventory readiness

  • Confirm a minimum 3-month supply of your hero SKU is on hand or inbound before launch
  • Set up FBT (Fulfilled by TikTok), Upgraded TikTok Shipping, or Collections by TikTok — Seller Shipping is no longer available for US sellers as of March 2026
  • Send sufficient inventory to the fulfillment method of choice before activating listings
  • Set reorder triggers to avoid stockouts — TikTok penalizes out-of-stock listings by suppressing organic reach for 14 days
  • Confirm return address and return policy parameters in Seller Center

Listing setup

  • Select 3–5 hero SKUs to lead with — do not list the full catalog on day one
  • Write product titles using buying-intent keywords, not brand-first language (see our TikTok Shop SEO guide for title formulas)
  • Keep titles under 80 characters and lead with the most searchable term
  • Upload a minimum of 5 high-resolution product images per SKU (white background + lifestyle)
  • Record or source at least one product video per hero SKU for the listing media gallery
  • Write the product description with benefit-first copy — what it does, who it is for, key ingredients or materials
  • Set pricing that reflects a credible offer — not artificially inflated to then discount
  • Configure variants (size, color, flavor) with accurate stock levels per variant
  • Assign the most specific accurate category and subcategory — wrong categorization kills search discovery regardless of title quality
  • Add all relevant product attributes in the listing form (brand, material, scent, skin type, etc.)

Shipping and policy setup

  • Configure shipping templates with accurate delivery windows — do not promise 2-day if fulfillment takes 5
  • Set return window and return policy in Seller Center (minimum 30 days recommended)
  • Confirm that packaging meets TikTok Shop shipping label requirements
  • Test one sample order end-to-end before going live if possible
The listing is a conversion asset, not a form to fill out. Every field in the listing affects how many people find you and how many of those convert. A weak title means less search traffic. A weak description means lower conversion. Lower conversion means the algorithm surfaces you less. Build the listing as if it has to sell on its own.

Phase 2: Creator seeding checklist (before go-live)

This phase runs in parallel with Phase 1. Start creator outreach as soon as your affiliate program is configured, but do not push creators to post until listings are live and inventory is confirmed.

Affiliate program configuration

  • Navigate to Seller Center → Growth → Affiliate Marketing and activate the Open Collaboration plan
  • Set Open Collaboration commission at 15–20% — this is the minimum range that attracts productive creators to a new brand
  • Create at least one Targeted Collaboration campaign for your hero SKU for direct creator invitations
  • Confirm sample eligibility settings — enable free samples for Targeted Collaboration creators
  • Set up the creator brief template with 3–5 core hooks your brand will use at launch

Creator identification and outreach

  • Use TikTok Creator Marketplace to identify 20–30 creators relevant to your category
  • Filter for creators who already make TikTok Shop content — they understand the format and convert better
  • Prioritize creators with 10K–200K followers over mega accounts for seeding — engagement rates and conversion are typically stronger
  • Check creator's recent content for average view counts — look for consistent performance, not one viral outlier
  • Send 10–15 Targeted Collaboration invitations before go-live
  • Include product samples with confirmed creators — ship immediately upon confirmation
  • Set clear posting timeline expectations (within 7–14 days of receiving sample)
  • Share the creative brief with each confirmed creator — do not leave content direction to chance
  • Establish usage rights terms in writing before samples ship
Expect 40–60% of invitations to result in posts. If you send 15 invitations, plan on 6–9 live videos in your first two weeks. That baseline is what generates early conversion signals and feeds the algorithm enough data to start placing your listing in more discovery surfaces. See our full creator outreach guide for messaging templates and selection criteria.
Open Collaboration

Any creator can apply to promote your product. Set commission, add product to the plan, and creators find you. Best for volume and passive inbound interest from creators already browsing TikTok Shop.

Targeted Collaboration

You invite specific creators directly. Better control, higher intent, and where your seeding budget goes. Use for your first 10–15 launch relationships. See our creator affiliate growth service.

Phase 3: Go-live day checklist

Go-live day is not the culmination — it is the starting gun. The actions you take (and do not take) on day one set the trajectory for the first two weeks.

Go-live day actions

  • Confirm all hero SKU listings are published and visible in the TikTok Shop storefront
  • Verify the affiliate dashboard is active and commission rates are correctly set
  • Confirm at least 2–3 seeded creators are confirmed and have received samples (posts expected within the week)
  • Review all listing titles, images, and descriptions one final time on mobile — this is how most buyers will see them
  • Confirm fulfillment is set up and a test order can be processed end-to-end
  • Set up your weekly reporting template (content volume, GMV by SKU, creator count, conversion rate)

What NOT to do on go-live day

  • Do not activate GMV Max campaigns — the account has no conversion data yet and the algorithm cannot optimize
  • Do not run Spark Ads yet — you need organic winners to amplify, and you do not have them yet
  • Do not launch more than 5 SKUs — spreading traffic dilutes conversion signals
  • Do not push creators to post immediately if samples have not arrived — poor content from an unboxing experience hurts more than delays

Phase 4: Week 1–2 optimization checklist

The first two weeks are about collecting signal, not scale. Your job is to understand what is working, fix what is not, and set up Week 3–4 for real activation.

Listing performance review

  • Review listing click-through rate (CTR) from search — below 2% indicates a title or thumbnail problem
  • Review add-to-cart rate — below 8% suggests the listing copy or imagery is not converting interest
  • Review product page conversion rate — identify which SKUs are underperforming and why
  • A/B test title variations if CTR is weak — small wording changes can produce 30–50% CTR improvements
  • Check whether products appear in relevant search results using the TikTok search bar

Creator and content review

  • Track all live creator videos — note view counts, click-through to shop, and conversion by video
  • Identify which hooks and angles are driving the most clicks to listing
  • Follow up with all creators who have not posted — offer help if the brief was unclear
  • Send the second batch of seeding invitations (10–15 additional creators) based on what you learned in batch one
  • Share top-performing video angles back to all confirmed creators as creative guidance for round two

Logistics and fulfillment review

  • Check fulfillment speed against promised delivery windows
  • Review any customer service tickets or complaints — address return requests promptly to protect seller score
  • Confirm inventory levels are adequate for projected Week 3–4 volume increase

Phase 5: Week 3–4 activation checklist

This is the phase where brands with a properly built foundation begin to pull away. GMV Max activation, Spark Ads, and paid amplification all become available — but only once the organic signals are real.

GMV Max activation criteria

  • Confirm you have at least 3 creator videos that have generated confirmed conversions (not just views)
  • Confirm your listing conversion rate is above 3% — below that, GMV Max budget will be wasted driving traffic to a weak page
  • Confirm inventory levels can sustain a 3–5x volume increase without stockout risk
  • If all three criteria are met: activate GMV Max with a modest daily budget and let it optimize for 5–7 days before scaling spend

Spark Ads setup

  • Identify the 1–2 creator videos with the strongest conversion signal from Week 1–2
  • Request Spark Ad authorization from those creators (or use content you hold usage rights to)
  • Set up Spark Ads with Product Sales objective, not Traffic or Awareness
  • Target similar audiences to the organic viewers who converted — not broad interest targeting
  • See our TikTok Shop Spark Ads service if you need help with campaign structure

Reporting setup

  • Configure weekly reporting dashboard: GMV by SKU, GMV by creator, CTR by listing, conversion rate, ROAS on paid
  • Set creator-level tracking to identify who drives repeat buyers vs. one-time conversions
  • Compare Week 3–4 performance against Week 1–2 baseline to confirm upward trajectory

Phase 6: First 90-day monthly review checklist

Most TikTok Shop brands plateau because they do not have a structured review cadence. Run these checks at the end of each of the first three months.

Month 1 review
  • Which hero SKU generated the most GMV?
  • Which creators drove the most conversions?
  • What hooks produced the highest CTR to listing?
  • Is listing conversion above 3%? If not, what changed?
  • Is inventory tracking ahead of projected sell-through?
Month 2 review
  • Are top creators from Month 1 still active?
  • Have you onboarded 5–10 new creators based on Month 1 data?
  • Is GMV Max spend returning positive ROAS?
  • Are Spark Ad campaigns beating organic-only baseline?
  • Have you expanded to a second SKU if Month 1 hero is healthy?

Month 3 review

  • Review total GMV trajectory — are you on pace for a meaningful run rate?
  • Review creator program: how many active creators, what is average GMV per creator?
  • Review listing SEO performance: are titles ranking in relevant searches? (See TikTok Shop SEO guide)
  • Review paid efficiency: what is blended ROAS across all paid channels?
  • Identify which SKUs should be expanded, scaled, or retired
  • Review competitor listings and creator activity — where are you losing ground?
  • Plan the next 90-day creator seeding pipeline with lessons from the first 90 days
What the first 90 days actually decide. The brands that hit $100K+ in their first quarter are not the ones who moved fastest. They are the ones who built the right foundation, activated channels in the right order, and iterated quickly on real conversion data. The beauty brand we scaled to $672K GMV in Year 1 followed this exact sequence — foundation first, amplification second.

The most common launch mistakes (and how to avoid them)

These are not edge cases. They are the errors we see repeatedly from brands launching without a structured checklist.

Activating GMV Max before organic data exists

GMV Max needs conversion signal to optimize. Launching it on a cold account produces poor results and wastes budget. Wait for 3+ converting organic videos before activating paid.

Going live with thin inventory

A viral video with no inventory is one of the most painful launch failures. TikTok also penalizes stockouts by suppressing organic reach for 14 days — a double punishment you cannot recover quickly from.

Launching the full catalog immediately

Spreading creator attention and traffic across 50 SKUs dilutes conversion signals on every listing. Start with 3–5 and build momentum on the hero before expanding.

Skipping the creative brief

Without a brief, creators default to generic unboxing content that performs weakly. The brief defines hooks, use-case framing, and the specific buying language that converts in your category. See our creator outreach guide for brief templates.

Frequently asked questions

How long does TikTok Shop seller verification take?
Verification typically takes 1–5 business days after you submit complete documentation. The most common reason for rejection is a mismatch between your business name on registration documents and on your bank account. Have both match exactly before you submit.
When should I activate GMV Max campaigns?
Do not activate GMV Max on go-live day. Wait until you have at least 3 converting organic creator videos confirmed. GMV Max needs conversion signal to optimize correctly. Activating it on a cold account with no data produces poor results and burns budget.
How many creators should I seed before launch?
Send 10–15 seeding invitations before go-live. Expect 40–60% to actually post. That gives you 5–8 live videos in the first week, which is the minimum needed to generate early conversion signals and a usable content baseline.
What commission rate should I set for the Open Collaboration plan?
Start at 15–20% for the Open Collaboration plan. This is the range that attracts productive creators to a new brand. You can adjust per creator with Targeted Collaboration once you identify top performers. Going below 10% on Open plan will severely limit creator participation.
How much inventory do I need before launching?
Maintain a minimum 3-month supply of your hero SKU before launch. TikTok penalizes out-of-stock listings by reducing organic reach for 14 days following a stockout. A viral creator video with no inventory is one of the most painful and avoidable launch failures.
