What does a TikTok Shop agency actually do?

A real TikTok Shop agency builds and operates a full commerce growth system — creator recruitment, affiliate management, listing optimization, Spark Ads, account operations, and reporting. Most brands have worked with social media agencies before. This is a different role with different accountability.

The short answer: seven interconnected functions

A TikTok Shop agency should be running seven core functions simultaneously. Most brands who hired an agency and felt disappointed found that their agency was only operating two or three of them — typically the visible-feeling ones like ads and seeding — while ignoring the unglamorous infrastructure that makes those inputs convert.

The US TikTok Shop market is projected at $23.4 billion in GMV for 2026, with brands winning market share not because they have bigger budgets, but because they have better-operated systems. Here is what those systems actually consist of.

What a functional agency delivers

Measurable GMV growth driven by a structured creator pipeline, optimized listings, paid amplification of proven content, and weekly data feedback that tells you what to do next.

What most brands get instead

A lot of activity — outreach volume, sample sends, ad spend — without the connective tissue that turns those inputs into compounding revenue. The difference is operational depth.

The 7 core functions of a TikTok Shop agency

1. Creator recruitment

This is the top of the funnel. A good agency does not blast every creator with a follower count above a threshold. They identify creators who have already made content in your product category — because creators who engage with adjacent products are 5–10x more likely to accept an affiliate offer and produce content that actually converts. Recruitment includes building prospect lists from TikTok's affiliate marketplace, manual search, and third-party tooling, then filtering by category fit, posting consistency, and buyer audience alignment.

The goal is not a large list of interested creators. The goal is an active roster of creators who post reliably, convert their audiences, and can be reactivated for new angles or Spark Ads authorization.

2. Affiliate management

Affiliate management is the operational backbone. Once creators are recruited and sampled, someone needs to be tracking commission structures (open vs. targeted collaboration), ensuring posting happens on schedule, chasing late deliverables, managing creator questions, and flagging which creators have earned expanded investment. This is not set-and-forget — it requires weekly touchpoints and a proper CRM or tracking system.

Commission rates vary meaningfully by category. In beauty and wellness, competitive affiliate commissions typically run 15–25%. Brands entering a new category should set slightly higher rates initially to buy velocity and reviews before normalizing as momentum builds.

3. Listing optimization

Most brands underinvest here. Listing optimization covers product titles (written to match actual search intent, not brand-speak), category selection, main images, the product description, bundle structuring, and pricing logic relative to competitors. A listing that converts at 3% instead of 6% costs you half your sales from every piece of creator content that drives traffic. The listing optimization service addresses all of these layers together.

Between 48% and 65% of TikTok Shop sales now come from in-app search, which means listing relevance and conversion quality are no longer secondary concerns — they directly determine how visible your products become.

4. Spark Ads management

Spark Ads are the mechanism for amplifying creator content that has already proven itself organically. An agency's job is to identify which pieces of organic affiliate content show strong completion rates and conversion signals, get the necessary creator authorization, and build paid campaigns that extend the reach of those proven assets. Spark Ads report 40–60% higher ROAS and 40% lower CPA compared to standard in-feed creative — but only when the source content is already good. The Spark Ads service is built around this sequence: validate organically first, then amplify.

5. Strategy and planning

Strategy is the decision layer that connects all other functions. It includes deciding which SKUs to prioritize, which creator tiers to weight at each GMV stage, how to sequence paid spend, when to run promotions, and how to respond when organic content underperforms. A strategy layer without execution is useless. Execution without strategy is random activity. Both need to be present.

6. Reporting and performance feedback

Reporting should tell you more than total GMV. A well-structured report shows creator activation rate (what percentage of enrolled creators actually posted — below 30% is a warning sign), content volume per week, GMV by creator tier, conversion rate by listing, and Spark Ads ROAS. The data from week three changes what you do in week four. Brands that get only monthly summaries are operating blind for most of the month.

7. Account operations

Seller Center operations covers the administrative layer: listing uploads and updates, compliance reviews, product approvals, promotional setup, customer message management, and platform policy monitoring. This is the least glamorous function and the one most likely to get dropped when an agency is thin. But compliance violations, delisted products, or stalled approvals can kill momentum at the worst time.

What does an agency actually do week to week vs month to month?

Understanding the cadence helps you hold an agency accountable.

Weekly activities

  • Creator outreach: new prospects sourced, messages sent, responses tracked
  • Sample coordination: approvals, tracking, confirmations
  • Content tracking: new posts live, performance by creator in first 48 hours
  • Spark Ads: new authorization requests, ad set performance review, budget adjustments
  • Seller Center: listing updates, customer message review, compliance flags
  • Weekly performance snapshot sent to the brand

Monthly activities

  • Creator roster review: who is earning re-investment, who has gone quiet
  • Listing audit: titles, images, and conversion rates reviewed against competitors
  • Commission structure review: are rates competitive for current GMV stage?
  • Strategy session: what is working, what is underperforming, what changes next month
  • Full performance report: GMV, creator count, content volume, ad ROAS, conversion rates

Avanta's approach to this full operational loop is documented in the TikTok Shop management service. The beauty brand Avanta managed to $672K GMV and 41,330 orders with a 7.49 ROI was run against this exact weekly cadence — the results came from consistency in execution, not from a single campaign.

What should you NOT expect a TikTok Shop agency to do?

Setting accurate expectations protects the relationship from the start. A TikTok Shop agency is an operating partner for your commerce channel — not a full brand partner.

Brand-side responsibilities

  • Brand positioning and messaging strategy
  • Product development and formulation decisions
  • Manufacturing timelines and inventory planning
  • Customer service infrastructure
  • Retail and wholesale channel management

Agency-side responsibilities

  • All TikTok Shop channel operations
  • Creator recruitment and affiliate management
  • Listing optimization and content coordination
  • Spark Ads strategy and management
  • Weekly reporting and performance iteration

The clearest sign of a broken agency relationship is scope confusion. When a brand expects an agency to fix a positioning problem through creator content — and the agency tries to deliver that — neither wins. The agency cannot write a brand strategy from the brief, and the brand gets frustrated when creator content does not fix a positioning gap that was never the agency's job to solve.

TikTok Shop agency vs social media agency: what is the actual difference?

This distinction matters more than most brands realize before they hire.

A social media agency measures success in reach, impressions, engagement rate, and follower growth. These are legitimate metrics for brand awareness objectives. A TikTok Shop agency measures success in GMV, conversion rate, cost per acquisition, creator activation rate, and ROAS. These are commerce metrics. The overlap in channel (TikTok) masks the fact that the job descriptions are entirely different.

Social media agencies typically excel at content calendars, community management, and building audiences. TikTok Shop agencies need to understand affiliate commission economics, listing algorithm behavior, Spark Ads mechanics, and how to build a creator roster that generates reliable purchase intent — not just views.

The wrong hire is expensive. Giving a social media agency your TikTok Shop account is like giving a brand designer your paid search account. The channel overlaps. The skill set does not. Ask any prospective agency to walk you through their affiliate activation rate tracking and their listing optimization process before signing.

How do you evaluate whether an agency is actually doing the work?

Operational transparency is the minimum standard. Any agency that has been running your account for 30 days should be able to answer these questions in under five minutes.

  • How many creators did you outreach to last week, and how many responded?
  • What is our current creator activation rate — and how does it compare to last month?
  • Which three pieces of creator content drove the most GMV this week?
  • What is our conversion rate on the hero listing, and what changed it?
  • Which Spark Ad sets are above target ROAS, and which are being paused?
  • What specific change are you making to the listing or creator brief next week based on what you learned?

Vague answers to concrete questions are the clearest signal of an agency that is reporting activity but not managing outcomes. See the full agency evaluation guide for a deeper framework.

Questions to ask a TikTok Shop agency before signing

These questions reveal operational depth quickly.

  • Can you show me a sample weekly report from an active client (redacted)?
  • What creator activation rate do you typically achieve in the first 60 days?
  • How do you decide which commission rate to set at launch vs at scale?
  • How do you determine which organic content to authorize for Spark Ads?
  • What does your listing optimization process look like — title, category, images?
  • What are your case studies, and can you walk me through the GMV growth timeline?
  • What does month one look like versus month three in terms of deliverables?

Good agencies answer these specifically. Weak agencies give you decks and testimonials instead of operational detail.

Red flags when evaluating TikTok Shop agencies

Process red flags

  • Cannot define or track creator activation rate
  • Pitches a flat commission rate without discussing category benchmarks
  • Leads with ad spend recommendations before establishing organic proof
  • Cannot explain the difference between open and targeted collaboration

Reporting red flags

  • Monthly-only reporting with no weekly data access
  • Reports that show reach and engagement but not GMV and conversion
  • No case studies with actual GMV numbers and timelines
  • Vague attribution ("our efforts drove awareness that led to sales")

Frequently asked questions

What does a TikTok Shop agency do?

A TikTok Shop agency manages the full growth system for a brand on TikTok Shop, including creator recruitment and affiliate management, product listing optimization, Spark Ads setup and management, account operations inside Seller Center, weekly performance reporting, and ongoing strategy iteration. Strong agencies handle all seven layers — not just one or two.

How is a TikTok Shop agency different from a social media agency?

A social media agency focuses on brand awareness, follower growth, and engagement metrics. A TikTok Shop agency is focused on in-app purchase conversion — recruiting commerce creators, optimizing product listings for search, managing affiliate commissions, running Spark Ads against proven creative, and driving measurable GMV. The performance accountability is entirely different.

What should a TikTok Shop agency NOT be expected to do?

A TikTok Shop agency should not be responsible for brand positioning strategy, product development decisions, manufacturing lead times, or customer service infrastructure. Those are brand-side responsibilities. The agency's job is to build and operate the commerce growth system on TikTok — not to define what you sell or why you exist.

How do you know if a TikTok Shop agency is actually doing the work?

Ask for weekly creator outreach numbers, activation rates, content volume, GMV by creator tier, and Spark Ads ROAS data. A working agency can show you these metrics broken down by week. If an agency gives you only monthly snapshots with vague attribution, that is a red flag. Operational transparency is the baseline standard.

What red flags should I watch for when evaluating a TikTok Shop agency?

Key red flags include agencies that cannot define creator activation rate, those who conflate TikTok content reach with purchase conversions, those without case studies showing GMV outcomes, and those who pitch a one-size commission rate without discussing category benchmarks. Also watch for agencies that lead with ads before establishing organic creator proof.

How much does a TikTok Shop agency charge?

Most TikTok Shop agencies charge a monthly management fee of $2,000–$8,000 plus a revenue share of 8–15% on Shop GMV. Some charge flat retainers only. The model varies by agency scope — a full-stack agency managing creator recruitment, ads, and listing optimization will typically cost more than one handling only affiliate outreach.
